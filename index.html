Detected Issues (3)

🚨
Direct DB Access
WebApp queries ProductsDB directly, bypassing ProductService. Exposes internal schema and removes service-level auth/validation.
Rule: clients must not access databases without a service intermediary.
⚠️
Circular Dependency
ProductService and InventoryService call each other. Cascading failures and deployment ordering become unpredictable.
Rule: service dependency graph must be a DAG — no cycles.
ℹ️
Missing Health Endpoint
Newly added SearchService has no GET /health route. The load balancer cannot detect unhealthy instances automatically.
Rule: all services must expose GET /health.

Scale Handling

The same PR at 35+ components. Two complementary strategies keep the diagram useful as the system grows.

Flat view: 35+ nodes — unnavigable Clustered views: 6–12 visible items — readable
Domain Clustering: Group all 35 services by bounded context. Each subgraph shows its services and databases. Changed domains are highlighted (amber border). Gives the big picture — who owns what and where cross-domain coupling lives.
Change-Focused: Collapse unchanged domains into single labeled boxes. Only the modified Catalog domain is expanded. Reviewers see exactly what changed and its immediate neighbors — nothing else. Reduces cognitive load from 35 nodes to 11.

Change-Focused View

Unchanged domains shrink to labeled stubs. Reviewers see only what's in the diff, plus one hop of context. Fastest for PR review — changed + neighbors only.

35 nodes → ~11 visible

Combined Strategy

Use domain clustering for the architecture overview, and switch to change-focused for each PR. The diff highlights which domains were touched, letting reviewers zoom in selectively.

Default: diff-focused
